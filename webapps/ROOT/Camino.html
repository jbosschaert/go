The Camino de Santiago

One trek that I would very much like to do in the future is the Camino de Santiago in Spain, and France. I have been thinking about this trek recently because my friend will be walking part of the trek soon! The Camino is a trek which is walked by many pilgrims, it is also known as "The Way of St. James", it can be begun in many different places, and many people start in France, they walk the "French Way" through the Pyrenees along the Camino de Compostela and always finish in the town of Santiago.

DurationRealistically it should take about 4 weeks to walk the entire trek
When To Go On TrekReally you can walk the Camino any time that suites you, many people prefer to walk in spring or autumn because it can get very hot during the summer. Some pilgrims choose to walk the Camino in winter. However this would be much harder than during spring/autumn/summer.
CountryFrance and Spain
AccommodationLodges (albergues or refugios)
However you can also take a more expesive option and stay in luxury hotels
Distance Travelednearly 800km
Start pointYou can start the Camino in many different places in Europe and there are many different routes which all eventually lead tot he same place, however the main start point is St. Jean in France.
End pointSantiago in Spain
